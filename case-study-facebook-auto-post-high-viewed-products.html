Case study

Facebook Auto Post Based on High-Viewed Products

The client needed a better way to turn product interest into timely social content. I built an automation workflow that identifies high-viewed products, prepares post content, and supports consistent publishing.

Book My Free Discovery Call Read the business problem
IndustryRetail and ecommerce
Company sizeGrowing SME
DepartmentMarketing and sales

Overview

Website demand signals became a practical social publishing workflow.

Instead of guessing which products to promote, the business could use product-view activity to identify what customers were already interested in and turn those signals into Facebook content.

The business problem

Marketing decisions were not connected to real product interest.

Staff had to manually decide which products to post, write captions, collect product details, and publish content. Good opportunities could be missed when high-interest products were not promoted quickly.

Manual product selection

The team had to review products and decide what to promote without a consistent interest signal.

Slow publishing

Preparing product copy and publishing posts took time away from higher-value marketing work.

Missed demand signals

Products receiving high attention were not always turned into timely social posts.

Challenges

The workflow needed automation without publishing irrelevant content.

  • Changing product interestProduct views could shift quickly based on campaigns, seasonality, or customer behavior.
  • Product data qualityPosts needed accurate product names, descriptions, prices, and links.
  • Brand consistencyAI-generated copy needed to follow a usable tone and avoid generic captions.
  • Publishing controlThe business needed the option to review content before it went live.

Solution

An automated product-to-Facebook publishing workflow.

I designed a workflow that checks product-view activity, identifies high-interest products, pulls product details, generates Facebook-ready captions, and prepares posts for publishing or review.

The goal was not to replace marketing judgment. The goal was to reduce repetitive publishing work and help the team act faster on products customers were already viewing.

System architecture

From product interest to publishable content.

The system connected website activity, product data, AI content generation, review rules, and Facebook publishing.

Business results

The team gained a more responsive publishing process.

  • More timely product promotionHigh-interest products could be identified and prepared faster.
  • Reduced manual publishing workStaff spent less time collecting details and drafting repeated post formats.
  • Better marketing consistencyPosts followed a repeatable workflow instead of one-off manual decisions.
  • Clearer operational visibilityThe team could see what was selected, drafted, reviewed, and published.

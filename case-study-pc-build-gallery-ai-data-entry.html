Case study

PC Build Gallery AI Data Entry

PC build gallery content needed to be cleaned, checked, and published quickly without sacrificing quality. I built an AI-assisted workflow that improves gallery metadata, validates photos, removes invalid images, generates polished setup visuals, and prepares entries for publishing.

IndustryRetail and PC hardware
Company sizeGrowing SME
DepartmentContent and operations

Overview

Manual gallery publishing became a quality-controlled content workflow.

The client needed a faster way to prepare PC build gallery entries while protecting accuracy, brand safety, image quality, and consistency.

The business problem

Each PC build required repeated cleanup, checking, and publishing work.

Staff had to clean up titles, descriptions, build tiers, tags, keywords, uploaded photos, and publishing details before each gallery entry could go live.

Manual data entry

Build specs had to be typed or cleaned up repeatedly before publishing.

Inconsistent entries

Gallery quality depended on who prepared the content and how much time they had.

Image preparation

Photos often needed enhancement or cleanup before they were ready to represent the build.

Unsafe content risk

Human-written fields needed to stay appropriate for a public retail website.

Invalid photo uploads

Some uploaded images could be unrelated, unclear, or not useful for a PC build gallery.

Publishing friction

Final gallery updates, photo uploads, and publishing steps created repeated admin work.

Challenges

The system needed to improve content quality without adding more admin work.

  • Varied input qualityBuild information and photos could arrive in different formats and quality levels.
  • Accuracy mattersIncorrect parts or specs would damage customer trust.
  • Visual presentationThe gallery needed images that made completed builds look polished.
  • Repeatable publishingThe workflow needed a consistent structure for every build entry.
  • Public content safetyTitles, quotes, descriptions, and tags needed to avoid inappropriate wording.
  • Photo validationThe system needed to detect whether uploaded images actually showed a PC, setup, laptop, or relevant build photo.
  • Generated image controlAI setup images needed to preserve the real PC as the hero subject and be clearly watermarked.

Solution

An AI-assisted gallery QA and publishing workflow.

The workflow receives a gallery request, checks that the payload is valid, retrieves the build details, improves the title, build tier, quote, description, tags, and searchable keywords using only the source data, then validates the written content for safety.

Uploaded photos are reviewed by AI to confirm they show a relevant PC build, computer setup, laptop, or assembled parts. Invalid photos can be separated and deleted before the gallery is finalized.

For stronger presentation, the system can pick the best build photo, generate polished setup-style visuals around the real PC, add an AI-generated watermark, upload the assets, and publish the gallery entry through the internal system.

System architecture

From raw build content to gallery-ready entries.

The workflow connects gallery intake, metadata improvement, written-content QA, photo validation, AI setup generation, asset upload, and publishing.

Business results

The gallery became easier to maintain and scale.

  • Reduced manual content prepStaff spent less time formatting repeated build information.
  • More consistent entriesGallery pages followed a clearer structure and presentation standard.
  • Better visual qualityImage enhancement helped builds look more polished before publishing.
  • Faster review processHuman attention shifted toward checking exceptions and accuracy.
  • Cleaner public galleryInvalid or unrelated images could be filtered before publishing.

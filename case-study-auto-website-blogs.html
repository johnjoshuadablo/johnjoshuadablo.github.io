Case study

Automated Website Blogs

The client needed a more consistent way to publish relevant hardware content for SEO. I built an AI-assisted workflow that reads hardware news feeds, filters out poor-fit topics, prepares blog metadata, generates paraphrased HTML content, writes it into the CMS, and publishes after success checks.

Overview

A slow content process became a repeatable SEO publishing workflow.

The system helped the client turn relevant hardware news into website-ready content without manually researching, filtering, formatting, and uploading every article from scratch.

The business problem

Consistent SEO content required too much manual research, filtering, and CMS work.

Publishing useful content takes topic monitoring, relevance checks, metadata preparation, article formatting, CMS creation, content upload, and publish confirmation. Without a system, the process becomes inconsistent.

Manual research

Staff had to monitor technology topics and decide what was worth writing about.

Inconsistent publishing

Content output depended on available time instead of a reliable workflow.

SEO opportunity cost

Relevant topics could be missed or published too late to support traffic growth.

Low-value topic risk

Deals, discounts, gaming-only stories, and clickbait topics could dilute the content strategy.

Formatting overhead

Each article needed clean HTML, source attribution, readable structure, and CMS-ready content.

Publishing reliability

The team needed confirmation that content was created, written successfully, and published in order.

Challenges

The workflow needed speed without low-quality AI content.

  • Source qualityThe system needed to work from credible topics and avoid low-value content scraping.
  • Brand relevanceNot every tech news item was useful for the client's audience.
  • Human reviewAI could assist drafting, but the business still needed editorial control.
  • SEO formattingPosts needed structure, titles, excerpts, and publishing metadata.
  • Topic filteringThe workflow needed to reject discount-heavy, promotional, software-only, entertainment, and clickbait items.
  • Originality and attributionGenerated articles needed to be paraphrased and attribute the source instead of copying publisher text.
  • CMS sequencingThe workflow had to create the blog record, write the article body, check success, wait, and publish in the correct order.

Solution

An AI-assisted RSS-to-CMS publishing pipeline with relevance checks.

The workflow runs on a schedule, reads a hardware news RSS feed, randomly selects an article, and uses AI to decide whether the topic is genuinely useful for a PC hardware audience.

If the topic passes, the system prepares a clean blog payload with a paraphrased title, short intro, author, content type, category, year, and thumbnail. It then creates the blog record in the CMS.

A second AI step generates a structured, paraphrased HTML article with source attribution, short factual sections, relevant images when available, and no CSS or markdown. The workflow writes the content, verifies the update, waits, then publishes.
